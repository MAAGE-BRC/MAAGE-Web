Epidemiology Investigation

The Epidemiology Investigation workflow provides a streamlined pipeline for genomic surveillance and outbreak analysis. Start from raw sequencing reads or assembled contigs, produce annotated genomes, identify clusters among public data, and perform in-depth comparative analyses -- all within a single integrated workspace.

How It Works

Begin by assembling and annotating your genome with Comprehensive Genome Analysis. Upload a raw read file (FASTQ) or start from assembled contigs. CGA will compute the assembly, annotate the genome, and provide a user-friendly summary of your organism.

If your organism is supported, allele calling is automatically performed by ChewBBACA followed by hierarchical clustering via HierCC (Hierarchical Clustering of cgMLST) against our public genome database. Use these cluster assignments to discover similar public genomes and identify potential outbreak connections.

The assembled genome becomes the jumping-off point for deeper analysis. From here you can launch any of the downstream services described below, as well as other genome-accepting and comparative genome services available on the platform [links to genome services and comparative services].

Investigation Services

Comprehensive Genome Analysis

Start Here

Upload raw reads or assembled contigs to produce an annotated genome. CGA performs assembly, annotation, and provides a comprehensive summary of your organism. For supported organisms, allele calling (ChewBBACA) and hierarchical clustering (HierCC) are run automatically against public genomes, giving you immediate cluster assignments to identify related isolates.

Core Genome MLST

Analyze a specific group of assembled and annotated genomes with ChewBBACA. Unlike the automatic clustering in CGA (which compares against all public genomes), this service lets you cluster only the genomes in your selected group -- mixing public and private data or analyzing exclusively private genomes. Produces a report detailing which genomes are similar and where they differ at the gene level. Trees are generated using GrapeTree's MSTree V2 algorithm.

Whole Genome SNP Analysis

Identify Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs) within a group of genomes using kSNP4. This service produces a report showing which genomes are similar and where they differ at the SNP level, organized into three categories: All SNPs identified across all genomes, Core SNPs present in every genome, and Majority SNPs present in a configurable percentage threshold. Compare genomes using a distance matrix and heatmap to understand shared and distinguishing variants. Supports Maximum Likelihood, Neighbor Joining, and Parsimony tree methods.

Phylogenetic Tree

Construct custom phylogenetic trees and map your metadata using the Codon Tree method. Visualize evolutionary relationships and transmission patterns across your investigation samples. Note that other services also produce trees: cgMLST generates trees via GrapeTree's MSTree V2 algorithm, and Whole Genome SNP Analysis produces trees using Maximum Likelihood, Neighbor Joining, and Parsimony methods.

Optional: Pre-Assembly Screening

Metagenomic Read Mapping

If you want to screen your raw reads before assembly, this service uses KMA to align reads against antibiotic resistance genes from CARD and virulence factors from VFDB. This is useful as a first-pass screen to identify resistance and virulence profiles directly from sequencing data, without waiting for a full assembly.

Getting started: Select a service from the sidebar on the left to open its submission form. Begin with Comprehensive Genome Analysis to assemble and annotate your genome. Once your genome is ready, use the downstream services to investigate clusters, identify SNPs, and build phylogenetic trees.
